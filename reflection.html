Reflection

Throughout my E-Portfolio, my goal was to make sure I highlighted my experiences and qualifications while still maintaining a consistent, modern theme. From chosing Github as a host for my site to the layout I used to display my projects, I tried to focus my audience on the aspects stated by this goal. There's also a few key aspects of my design that are specifically included to show my audience this like my skills section, the intro to the About Me page, and my images on the Projects page.
I choose to use Github as my host as opposed to Wix or Penn State Sites as all of my code and write-ups are already on my profile, allowing a viewer to easily navigate to my code. As a Computer Science student, I want my audience to be focused on the work I've produced and how it's shaped my interest and code throughout the years, and where I intend on continuing into the field. Using Github pages, which is 100% free, I can have everything I'd want a recruiter or technical employee in one place to show my efficiency. The website itself has a few key aspects I specifically include to try and meet this goal.
The landing page for my site has one of my favorite aspects of it. The left column that shows my expertise in multiple skills is an efficient and concise way of giving my audience a rundown of my qualifications.
